Angel Biscuits
Makes: 24 biscuits
Temp: 400°F
Time: 12 min
Ingredients
  • 5 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup vegetable shortening
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 1 pkg dry yeast
  • ½ cup warm water
  • 2 cups buttermilk
Instructions
  1. Sift all dry ingredients together into a large bowl.
  2. Cut in shortening thoroughly until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Dissolve yeast in warm water. Add yeast mixture and buttermilk to flour mixture.
  4. Mix with a spoon until all flour is moistened. Cover bowl and refrigerate until needed (can be kept several days).
  5. Remove as much dough as needed. Roll on a floured board to ½ inch thick.
  6. Cut with biscuit cutter. Bake at 400°F for 12 minutes until golden.
🧡 Greer Tip: Keep the dough in the fridge for up to a week and bake fresh each morning. On Christmas day, pop them in the oven while gifts are being opened — the whole house will smell incredible.
Christmas French Toast
Serves: 8
Temp: 400°F
Time: 25 min bake
Ingredients
  • 1 loaf French bread
  • 5 eggs
  • ¾ cup milk
  • 1 tbsp pure vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • 4 ripe bananas, sliced
  • 1 (20 oz) bag strawberries
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp apple pie spice
  • Cinnamon sugar, for topping
Instructions
  1. Cut French bread into 8 thick slices.
  2. Whisk together eggs, milk, baking powder, and vanilla. Pour over bread slices. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  3. In the morning, combine strawberries, bananas, sugar, and apple pie spice in a large bowl.
  4. Spread fruit mixture into a greased baking dish. Top with the egg-soaked bread slices.
  5. Sprinkle generously with cinnamon sugar.
  6. Bake at 400°F for 20–25 minutes until golden and set.
🧡 Greer Tip: Prep this entirely the night before and slide it in the oven on Christmas morning while everyone opens gifts. By the time the chaos settles, breakfast is ready.
Eggnog French Toast
Serves: 4
Prep: 10 min
Cook: 15 min
Ingredients
  • 3 large eggs, beaten
  • 1½ cups eggnog
  • 1½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 2½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp light rum
  • 1 loaf day-old challah bread, sliced 1-inch thick
  • Butter, for the pan
  • Powdered sugar and syrup, to serve
📷
Add your photo here
Instructions
  1. Whisk together eggs, eggnog, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and rum in a medium bowl.
  2. Heat a lightly buttered skillet or cast iron pan over medium heat.
  3. Dip challah slices one at a time into the eggnog custard, letting each side soak for about 20 seconds.
  4. Fry each slice on both sides until golden brown, about 3–4 minutes per side.
  5. Serve hot dusted with powdered sugar, with butter and warm syrup on the side.
🧡 Greer Tip: Day-old challah is the secret — it absorbs the custard without falling apart. Store leftovers in an airtight container up to 3 days; reheat in the oven at 350°F for 8 minutes. (Recipe: Grandbaby Cakes)
Strawberry Stuffed French Toast
Serves: 6
Prep: 10 min
Cook: 10 min
Ingredients
  • 8 oz whipped cream cheese
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • ¼ cup strawberries, diced (plus more for serving)
  • 12 slices brioche bread (1 loaf)
  • 1 cup milk
  • 4 eggs
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • Maple syrup & powdered sugar, to serve
📷
Add your photo here
Instructions
  1. Combine whipped cream cheese, honey, and diced strawberries in a bowl. Set aside.
  2. Whisk together milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt in a shallow bowl.
  3. Spread the cream cheese mixture on 6 brioche slices; seal each with a second slice.
  4. Heat a skillet over medium heat and melt ½ tbsp butter.

  6. Serve with extra strawberries, maple syrup, and powdered sugar.

Monkey Bread
Serves: 16
Temp: 350°F
Time: 35–45 min
Ingredients
  • 4 tubes (7.5 oz each) refrigerated buttermilk biscuits
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • ½ cup packed brown sugar
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Generously grease a 9- or 10-inch fluted tube (Bundt) pan.
  2. Cut each biscuit into 4 pieces and roll into balls.
  3. Combine granulated sugar and cinnamon in a zip-lock bag. Add biscuit balls in batches and shake to coat.
  4. Arrange cinnamon-sugar balls evenly in the prepared pan. Sprinkle any remaining cinnamon sugar over the top.
  5. Mix melted butter and brown sugar together; pour evenly over the biscuit balls.
  6. Place pan on a baking sheet. Bake 35–45 minutes until golden brown and cooked through. Cool 5 minutes, then invert onto a serving platter.
🧡 Greer Tip: Let the kids roll the dough balls — this is a recipe they can actually help with! Pull apart with your hands at the table. It is meant to be messy and fun.
Maple-Pepper Bacon
Serves: 6–8
Temp: 375°F
Time: 32 min
Ingredients
  • 1 lb thick-cut bacon
  • Pure maple syrup, for brushing
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
📷
Add your photo here
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Position wire racks on 2 rimmed baking sheets.
  2. Lay bacon in a single layer on the racks.
  3. Bake 7 minutes. Remove from oven and brush generously with maple syrup; season with cracked black pepper.
  4. Return to oven. Continue baking, flipping and brushing with syrup and seasoning with pepper every 5 minutes, until caramelized and glossy — about 25 more minutes.
  5. Let cool 5 minutes on the racks before serving (the glaze firms up as it cools).

Breakfast Sausage Casserole
Serves: 10–12
Temp: 375°F
Time: 1 hour
Ingredients
  • 1 lb sage-flavored breakfast sausage
  • 3 cups shredded hash brown potatoes, drained & pressed
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • 12 oz mild Cheddar cheese, shredded
  • ½ cup onion, shredded
  • 1 (16 oz) container small-curd cottage cheese
  • 6 jumbo eggs
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly grease a 9×13-inch baking dish.
  2. Brown sausage in a skillet over medium-high heat. Drain, crumble, and set aside.
  3. Stir together shredded potatoes and melted butter. Press mixture into the bottom and up the sides of the baking dish to form a crust.
  4. In a bowl, mix sausage, Cheddar, onion, cottage cheese, and eggs together. Pour over potato crust.
  5. Bake 1 hour or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Let cool 5 minutes before serving.
🧡 Greer Tip: Assemble the night before, cover, and refrigerate. In the morning, bake straight from the fridge — just add 10 extra minutes. This is the definition of a stress-free holiday morning.
Cottage Cheese Egg Bites
Makes: 7 bites
Ninja Foodi / Instant Pot
Time: 20 min
Ingredients
  • 6 large eggs
  • ½ cup small-curd cottage cheese
  • ½ cup Gruyere or sharp cheddar, shredded
  • 4 strips bacon, cooked & crumbled
  • ¼ cup roasted red peppers, diced
  • Salt, pepper & garlic powder to taste
  • 1 cup water (for Ninja Foodi pot)
  • Cooking spray
📷

Instructions
  1. Add 1 cup water to the Ninja Foodi pot. Insert the reversible rack in the low position.
  2. Blend eggs and cottage cheese in a blender until completely smooth, about 30 seconds.
  3. Stir in shredded cheese, bacon, roasted peppers, and seasonings.

  7. Carefully remove molds. Let sit 2 minutes, then unmold and serve.
🧡 Greer Tip: These keep in the fridge for 5 days — make a batch on Sunday and reheat all week. Swap in any cheese or veggies you love.

Spicy Sausage Balls
Makes: ~36 balls
Temp: 325°F
Time: 15 min
Ingredients
  • ½ lb ground pork sausage (regular)
  • ½ lb ground spicy pork sausage
  • 3 oz processed cheese sauce (Velveeta)
  • 2 cups buttermilk biscuit dry mix
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine regular sausage, spicy sausage, cheese sauce, and biscuit mix.
  3. Mix well with your hands until fully combined.
  4. Roll into small balls, about 1 to 1½ inches in diameter. Place on an ungreased baking sheet.
  5. Bake 15 minutes or until fully cooked through and lightly golden.
  6. Serve warm with toothpicks. Ranch or honey-mustard on the side is a great addition.
🧡 Greer Tip: These disappear faster than any other dish at the party. Always double the recipe. You can freeze unbaked balls and bake from frozen — just add 5 minutes.
Sugar & Nut Glazed Brie
Serves: 8–10
Temp: 500°F
Time: 7 min
Ingredients
  • ¼ cup packed brown sugar
  • ¼ cup chopped macadamia nuts or pecans
  • 1 tbsp brandy
  • 1 (14 oz) round of Brie cheese
  • Apple wedges, for serving
  • Pear wedges, for serving
  • 2–3 tbsp lemon juice (to brush fruit)
  • Crackers, for serving
Instructions
  1. Stir together brown sugar, chopped nuts, and brandy in a small bowl. Cover and chill at least 24 hours (up to 1 week).
  2. Preheat oven to 500°F.
  3. Place Brie on an oven-safe platter or pie plate. Bake 4–5 minutes until the cheese is just slightly softened.
  4. Spread the sugar-nut mixture evenly over the top of the warm Brie.
  5. Return to oven and bake 2–3 more minutes, until the sugar melts and bubbles.
  6. Brush fruit wedges with lemon juice. Arrange fruit on one side of the Brie, crackers on the other. Serve immediately.
🧡 Greer Tip: Make the topping up to a week ahead — it only improves. Serve immediately after the second bake; the cheese firms back up as it cools. Beautiful on a holiday cheese board.
Cranberry-Pear Relish
Makes: ~3 cups
Chill: 3 hrs
Stovetop
Ingredients
  • 2 cups sugar
  • ½ cup orange juice
  • ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 piece (1 inch) peeled fresh ginger
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bags (12 oz each) fresh cranberries
  • 2 firm ripe Bosc or Anjou pears, peeled, cored & cubed small
📷
Add your photo here
Instructions
  1. In a 4-quart saucepan, combine sugar, orange juice, balsamic vinegar, ginger, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally.
  2. Add cranberries and pears. Return to a boil, stirring frequently.
  3. Reduce heat to medium-low. Cook 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until most cranberries have popped, pears are tender, and relish has thickened slightly.
  4. Discard ginger piece and cinnamon stick.
  5. Spoon into a serving bowl. Cover and refrigerate at least 3 hours before serving.
  6. Keeps refrigerated up to 4 days. (Source: Good Housekeeping)
🧡 Greer Tip: Make this a week ahead — it only gets better. The balsamic gives it an elegant depth. Incredible alongside the Thanksgiving turkey and just as good on a biscuit the next morning.
Black Bean Salad with Lime Vinaigrette

No cook
Time: 15 min
Ingredients
  • 2 (15 oz) cans black beans, rinsed & drained
  • 1 (15 oz) can whole kernel corn, drained
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
  • ½ red onion, finely diced
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • 1 avocado, diced (optional, add at serving)
📷
Add your photo here
Instructions
  1. Combine black beans, corn, red bell pepper, and red onion in a large bowl.

Six Can Chicken Tortilla Soup
Serves: 6
Stovetop
Time: 20 min
Ingredients
  • 2 (14.5 oz) cans chicken broth
  • 1 (15 oz) can whole kernel corn, drained
  • 1 (15 oz) can black beans
  • 1 (10 oz) can chunk chicken
  • 1 (10 oz) can diced tomatoes with green chile peppers, drained
  • Crushed tortilla chips, for serving
  • Shredded Cheddar cheese, for serving
Instructions
  1. Pour chicken broth, corn, black beans, chunk chicken, and diced tomatoes with green chiles into a large saucepan or stockpot over medium heat.
  2. Stir to combine all ingredients.
  3. Simmer, stirring occasionally, until chicken is heated through, about 15 minutes.
  4. Serve hot, topped with crushed tortilla chips and shredded Cheddar cheese.
🧡 Greer Tip: Stir in a cup of cooked rice for a chunkier, heartier version. Finish each bowl with a squeeze of lime, a dollop of sour cream, and chopped cilantro for a restaurant-quality finish. This is the easiest weeknight meal in the book. (Source: Allrecipes)
Delilah's Macaroni and Cheese
Serves: 20+
Temp: 325°F
Time: 1 hour
Ingredients
  • 2 lbs elbow macaroni
  • 12 eggs
  • 1 cup cubed Velveeta
  • ½ lb (2 sticks) butter, melted
  • 6 cups half-and-half, divided
  • 4 cups grated sharp yellow Cheddar, divided
  • 2 cups grated extra-sharp white Cheddar
  • 1½ cups grated mozzarella
  • 1 cup grated Asiago
  • 1 cup grated Gruyere
  • 1 cup grated Monterey Jack
  • 1 cup grated Muenster
  • ⅛ tsp salt; 1 tbsp black pepper
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 325°F. Cook macaroni in salted water until just al dente, about 10 minutes. Drain and set aside warm.
  2. Whisk eggs in a very large bowl until frothy.
  3. Add Velveeta, butter, and 2 cups half-and-half to the eggs. Add warm macaroni and toss until cheese melts and mixture is smooth.
  4. Add remaining 4 cups half-and-half, 3 cups sharp yellow Cheddar, all remaining cheeses, salt, and pepper. Toss to combine.
  5. Divide into 3 (3-quart) baking dishes. Bake 30 minutes.
  6. Top with remaining 1 cup sharp yellow Cheddar. Bake another 30 minutes until golden.
🧡 Greer Tip: This is THE mac. No shortcuts. This is what the holidays taste like in this family. Do not skip any cheese. Do not substitute. Just make it.
Patti LaBelle's Mac and Cheese
Serves: 8
Temp: 350°F
Time: 45 min
Ingredients
  • 1 lb elbow macaroni
  • 8 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • ½ cup shredded Muenster cheese
  • ½ cup shredded mild Cheddar
  • ½ cup shredded sharp Cheddar
  • ½ cup shredded Monterey Jack
  • 2 cups half-and-half
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup Velveeta, cubed small
  • ¼ tsp seasoned salt
  • ⅛ tsp freshly ground black pepper
📷

Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease a 9×13-inch baking pan.
  2. Combine Muenster, mild Cheddar, sharp Cheddar, and Monterey Jack in a bowl. Set aside.

Spicy Sausage Stuffing
Serves: 8–10
Temp: 350°F
Time: 35 min
Ingredients
  • 1 lb spicy pork sausage
  • ½ onion, chopped
  • 1 cup celery, chopped
  • 3 cups stuffing cubes
  • 4 cups additional bread cubes
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 can cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 tbsp parsley, chopped
  • ½ tsp salt (optional)
  • 1 tsp poultry seasoning
  • Butter, for the casserole dish
Instructions
  1. Crumble and brown sausage in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add onion and celery; cook until tender, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add 3 cups bread cubes to the skillet; cook until lightly browned.
  4. In a saucepan, heat milk and soup together, stirring until smooth.
  5. Add 4 additional cups stuffing cubes to the soup mixture, then combine with the sausage mixture. Add parsley, salt, and poultry seasoning. Mix well.
  6. Place in a buttered casserole dish. Cover and bake at 350°F for 25 minutes. Uncover and bake 10 more minutes.
🧡 Greer Tip: Use a spicy Italian sausage for extra kick, or go mild if kids are at the table. This stuffing is good enough to be the main dish. Do not wait for turkey season to make it.
Kicked Up Bacon Cheese Mashed Potatoes

Stovetop
Time: 35 min

  • 4 russet potatoes (~3 lbs), peeled & cubed 1-inch
  • 1¾ tsp salt, divided
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 8 slices bacon, cooked crisp & crumbled
  • ½ lb sharp Cheddar, grated
  • ¼ cup sour cream
  • ¼ cup fresh chives, chopped
📷

Instructions
  1. Place potatoes and 1 tsp salt in a heavy pot. Cover with water by 1 inch. Bring to a boil, then simmer until fork-tender, about 20 minutes.

Cioppino — Seafood Stew
Serves: 8
Stovetop
Time: 1.5 hrs
Ingredients
  • 2 (28 oz) cans whole San Marzano tomatoes
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 large fennel bulb, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 2 tsp fennel seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable or fish stock
  • 1½ lbs mussels, scrubbed
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled & deveined
  • 1 lb cod or halibut, cut in 2-inch pieces
  • Tarragon or parsley, crusty bread to serve
Instructions
  1. Crush tomatoes by hand in a large bowl until no large pieces remain. Set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high. Add onion, fennel, and garlic; season with salt and pepper. Cook 8–10 minutes until softened and starting to brown.
  3. Add fennel seeds and bay leaves; stir and cook 1–2 minutes.
  4. Add white wine and crushed tomatoes. Cook 10–15 minutes to concentrate flavors.
  5. Add stock. Bring to a simmer; cook uncovered 45 minutes. Season throughout.
  6. Increase heat to medium. Add mussels; cook 5 minutes until just opening. Add shrimp and cod; cook 4–5 more minutes until all seafood is cooked through.
  7. Serve from the pot with crusty bread and fresh herbs.
🧡 Greer Tip: Make the tomato-fennel base a day ahead — it is even better on day 2. Add the seafood fresh right before serving. (Source: BuzzFeed / Alison Roman)

Bacardi Rum Cake
Serves: 16
Temp: 325°F
Time: 1 hour
Cake Ingredients
  • 1 cup chopped pecans or walnuts
  • 1 (18.5 oz) pkg yellow cake mix
  • 1 (3.75 oz) pkg vanilla instant pudding
  • 4 eggs
  • ½ cup cold water
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • ½ cup Bacardi dark rum
Glaze
  • ¼ lb (1 stick) butter
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup Bacardi dark rum
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 325°F. Grease and flour a 12-cup Bundt pan. Sprinkle nuts over the bottom.
  2. Beat together cake mix, pudding, eggs, water, oil, and rum until smooth. Pour batter over nuts.
  3. Bake 1 hour. Cool 10 minutes, then invert onto a serving plate.

  3. Add spices, vanilla and a pat of margarine; stir gently. Do not over stir.
  4. Add torn bread pieces last. If mixture appears too dry, add ½ cup milk.
  5. Pour into prepared pan. Bake at 350° for 1 hour.
  6. Serve warm with a drizzle of bourbon sauce or vanilla cream.
Greer Tip: Use day-old bread — it absorbs the custard far better than fresh. The more stale, the better the pudding.
Monkey Bread
Breakfast / Dessert · Bundt pan · 30–40 min
By Vonretta Singleton
Ingredients
  • 4 cans buttermilk biscuits
  • ¾ c. sugar
  • 1 tsp. cinnamon
  • ½ c. chopped pecans
Topping
  • 1 c. sugar
  • 1½ tsp. cinnamon
  • 1 stick oleo (butter)
  • ¼ c. brown sugar
Instructions
  1. Cut each biscuit into quarters.
  2. Mix ¾ c. sugar and 1 tsp. cinnamon together in a bowl.
  3. Grease or spray a bundt pan well.
  4. Roll each biscuit piece in the cinnamon-sugar mixture.
  5. Layer the coated pieces in the bundt pan, sprinkling pecans throughout the layers.
  6. For the topping: combine 1 c. sugar, 1½ tsp. cinnamon, oleo and brown sugar in a small saucepan. Heat to boiling, stirring constantly.
  7. Remove topping from heat and let cool slightly.
  8. Pour the warm topping over the biscuit pieces in the pan.
  9. Bake at 350° for 30–40 minutes until deep golden brown.
  10. Cool in pan for 5 minutes only, then invert onto a serving plate while still warm.
Greer Tip: Don't let it cool too long in the pan — the caramel will set and stick! Invert quickly after 5 minutes and watch it all come tumbling out.

Baked Brie en Croûte
Appetizer · 350° · 40 min · Serves 2–4
Ingredients
  • 1 wheel Double Cream Brie
  • ⅓ cup walnuts, roughly chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, destemmed and chopped
  • 1 tbsp honey, plus more for drizzling
  • 1 package crescent roll dough
  • Crackers or sliced baguette, for serving
Variations: Swap walnuts for pecans. Try fig jam instead of honey. Add a sprinkle of rosemary for a savory note.
Instructions
  1. Preheat oven to 350°.
  2. Unwrap the Brie. Using a knife, score the top of the rind with a large, shallow "X."
  3. In a small bowl, combine walnuts, chopped thyme and honey.
  4. Unroll crescent dough and carefully separate the 8 triangles along the perforated seams.
  5. On a parchment-lined baking sheet, arrange the triangles in a "star" shape — shortest sides slightly overlapping at the center so the parchment shows through the middle.
  6. Place Brie in the center of the star.
  7. Top the Brie with the walnut and honey mixture.
  8. Fold the crescent triangles up and over the Brie, pressing gently to seal.

Mom's White Sandwich Bread
Bread · Two loaves · 400° · 40–50 min
From Mom's handwritten card
Ingredients
  • 1 c. (250ml) warm water
  • 1 pkg (¼ oz / 7g) active dry yeast
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3½ c. all-purpose flour (plus up to ½ c. more)
  • 2 tbsp butter or shortening (softened)
Instructions
  1. Dissolve yeast in warm water with sugar. Let stand until foamy, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add salt and butter to yeast mixture. Stir in flour gradually, combining to form a soft dough.
  3. Turn onto floured surface; knead until smooth and elastic, about 8–10 minutes. If sticky, knead in up to ½ cup more flour.
  4. Place in a greased bowl, turning once to coat. Cover; let rise in warm place about 1 hour, until doubled.
  5. Punch down dough. Divide in 2 equal parts; shape into smooth balls. Let rest 10 minutes.
  6. Shape into 2 loaves; place in greased 9×5-inch pans. Cover; let rise 45 min in warm place.
  7. Preheat oven to 400° (200°C). Bake 40–50 minutes until golden. Cool on rack.
Greer Tip: From Mom's handwritten card — her simple everyday bread. Perfect toasted with butter, or as the base for a spectacular French toast.
Stephen & Wend's Oatmeal-Raisin Cookies
Cookies · Classic family recipe
From the handwritten recipe card
Ingredients
  • 1 c. shortening
  • 1 c. sugar
  • 1 c. brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • 1½ c. flour
  • 1 tsp. baking soda
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 3 c. rolled oats
  • 1 c. raisins
Instructions
  1. Cream together shortening, sugar and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  2. Mix in 2 eggs and vanilla.
  3. Sift together flour, baking soda and salt; fold the flour mixture into the creamed mixture until just combined.
  4. Stir in oats and raisins.
  5. Roll into small balls and place on a greased cookie sheet, spacing evenly.
  6. Bake at 350° for 10–12 minutes until edges are set and centers are just barely done. Do not overbake — they firm as they cool.
  7. Cool on the pan 5 minutes before transferring to a rack.
Greer Tip: Stephen and Wend's secret is in the shortening — it keeps these cookies thick and chewy rather than crisp and flat. A family staple for a reason.
