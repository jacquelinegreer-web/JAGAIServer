CliftonStrengths · July 2026
Learn the Framework. Speak the Language.
Free · Gallup-produced content · No certification required yet
What to Do
Subscribe to Gallup Called to Coach on YouTube — this is Gallup's own coaching curriculum, free and ungated. Start with the themes most common in your client profiles
youtube.com → Gallup Called to Coach
Subscribe to The CliftonStrengths Podcast — Gallup's official theme-by-theme deep dives. Listen during commutes and workouts
Apple Podcasts / Spotify → The CliftonStrengths Podcast
Sign up for Gallup's free coaching webinars — they run periodic free online learning events. Go to gallup.com/learning and subscribe to email alerts
Run yourself through a fresh reflection on your own Top 5 through a coaching lens — not just "what am I good at" but "how do these show up under pressure?" Write it out. This is your first coaching session.
Conduct one informal CliftonStrengths debrief with a trusted peer or colleague this month — use the structure from Called to Coach. Document what worked and what felt uncertain.
What You're Building
Fluency in the 34 themes — not just the names, but the coaching implications and shadow sides
A reliable debrief structure you can use with clients before you are certified
The vocabulary to speak credibly to corporate buyers who have already administered CliftonStrengths inside their organizations

Cost: $0

360 Feedback · August 2026
Run Your Own 360. Practice the Debrief.
Free tools · Spidergap free tier · No certification required yet
What to Do
Run yourself through a 360 first. Use Spidergap's free tier (up to 5 feedback recipients, real anonymity, PDF output). Send to 4–5 colleagues who know your leadership well. Sit with the data before you debrief anyone else.
spidergap.com → Free for up to 5 recipients
Design a simple leadership competency 360 template using Google Forms or Spidergap for early client work. Use 10–12 questions max: self-rating, rater rating, open-ended themes. This is legitimate for early-stage practice.
Conduct one practice 360 debrief with a trusted peer, colleague, or pro-bono client — someone who has received multi-rater feedback before and can give you honest feedback on your facilitation
Review CCL's free downloadable 360 Assessment Selection Guide — it maps assessment tools to use case, client level, and org context. Helps you speak knowledgably about the landscape before you're certified.
ccl.org → Free 360 Assessment Selection Guide (PDF)
Read the CCL article: "How to Implement an Organizational 360 Feedback Initiative" — free, research-backed, and gives you the language enterprise HR buyers use
What You're Building
A tested 360 process you can sell before you have a named certification — your lived experience designing and running 360s is already real; this formalizes your approach
A debrief structure that is yours: how you open the conversation, how you hold the data, how you move from insight to action
Familiarity with the tool landscape so you can make an informed decision between CCL, Korn Ferry 360, and Leadership Circle Profile when you invest
One documented case to reference in client conversations and on your website
Cost: $0
Milestone: One 360 debrief conducted and documented
Coaching Practice · July–August 2026
Start Coaching. Document Everything.
Pro-bono and early clients · Hours begin counting now
What to Do
Conduct 2–3 pro-bono "discovery sessions" with leaders in your network — use the GROW model as your structure. These are real coaching conversations, not informational interviews.
Log every session from day one: date, duration, client (anonymized), session focus, what you tried, what landed. This is your hours log and it counts toward ACC.
Read Co-Active Coaching (library copy or borrow) — do not wait until you're enrolled in ICF training. The framework will make your early sessions more intentional.
Read The Coaching Habit — one sitting, this week. Know the 7 questions cold before you enter your first session.
Sign up for ICF's free membership tier and explore the ICF Competency Framework. Download the ACC application requirements so you know exactly what you're building toward.
coachingfederation.org → ICF membership + ACC requirements
Why This Matters
Hours logged in July and August count toward your ACC total. Every session before ICF training officially starts is already building your credential.
Pro-bono work in this phase is not a concession — it is the investment. The leaders you coach now become your first testimonials, referrals, and case studies.
Starting before you're certified forces you to coach from your genuine instincts and existing frameworks. That is actually better training than waiting for permission.
Cost: $0 (books from library)
Milestone: 10+ hours logged, hours doc started
What Comes Next
After the Free Ramp, you move into Phase 1 (September 2026) with ICF Level 1 enrollment, insurance, and your first paying clients. By then you will already have coaching hours, a tested 360 process, and real CliftonStrengths debrief experience. The certifications you pay for will build on a foundation you have already laid — not fill a vacuum.

05 · Reading List
Core Library for Coaching Practice
Read in order of the phases above. The right book at the right moment in your practice lands differently than reading ahead of experience. Nine books across one year is one every six weeks — that is the right pace.
Co-Active Coaching
Kimsey-House, Kimsey-House, Sandahl & Whitworth
Phase 1 · July 2026
The foundational text behind the Co-Active model. Read it in Phase 1 — before you accumulate habits from other frameworks. One of the most cited books in ICF-aligned training programs.
The Coaching Habit
Michael Bungay Stanier
Phase 1 · August 2026
Seven core coaching questions. Simple, practical, frequently referenced. Read it fast — one sitting. Know the vocabulary before your clients bring it up.
Strengths-Based Leadership
Tom Rath & Barry Conchie
Phase 1 · September 2026
Companion to CliftonStrengths — links strengths to follower needs and leadership outcomes. Essential for making your StrengthsFinder debriefs land with senior leaders, not just as personal discovery.
What Got You Here Won't Get You There
Marshall Goldsmith
Phase 2 · October 2026
Core reading for stakeholder-centered coaching. Understand the behavioral change model before you study the full Goldsmith methodology. This is the book your C-suite clients will have already read.
Immunity to Change
Robert Kegan & Lisa Laskow Lahey
Phase 2 · November 2026
The most rigorous framework for persistent leadership barriers. Read it before you take the facilitator training. Understanding the theory first makes the training land at a different level.
Dare to Lead
Brené Brown
Phase 3 · January 2027
Widely referenced by executive clients. Know the vocabulary — shame resilience, rumbling with vulnerability, armored vs. daring leadership. You will hear it in sessions whether you assigned it or not.
Multipliers
Liz Wiseman
Phase 3 · February 2027
Widely used framework in education and nonprofit leadership coaching. Know it cold — Multiplier vs. Diminisher is a lens many of your clients will find immediately useful and self-implicating.
Leadership and Self-Deception
The Arbinger Institute
Phase 3 · March 2027
Powerful for clients stuck in self-justifying patterns. Written as a narrative, not a textbook — accessible and frequently cited. The "in the box / out of the box" framing is immediately applicable in coaching sessions.
Radical Candor
Kim Scott
Phase 1 · September 2026
The framework your manager-level clients need most and quote least. Care personally, challenge directly — the four quadrants (Radical Candor / Ruinous Empathy / Obnoxious Aggression / Manipulative Insincerity) are immediately applicable in session. Read it before you teach it. You will recognize yourself and your clients in every chapter.
The Five Dysfunctions of a Team
Patrick Lencioni
Phase 2 · November 2026
Written as a business fable — readable in a single sitting. The five-level model (absence of trust, fear of conflict, lack of commitment, avoidance of accountability, inattention to results) gives your Group Cohort sessions a shared diagnostic framework. Your clients will have already heard of it; you need to know it cold to coach into it, not just reference it.
The Work of Leaders
Grenny, Patterson et al.
Phase 4 · May 2027
Companion to Everything DiSC Work of Leaders — relevant when you eventually add DISC to your toolkit. Also valuable standalone as a framework for vision, alignment, and execution coaching conversations.

Georgetown Institute for Transformational Leadership
Georgetown SCS → ITL
 ICF Level 1 Program The DC-market credential that Reem Labib Tyson holds. ICF Level 1 accredited. Strong alumni network in the DMV. If you want institutional brand recognition in the DC coaching market, this is the name that lands. Research as an alternative to other ICF Level 1 programs.
ICF Mentor Coaching Directory
coachingfederation.org → Find a Coach
 Directory Use this to find your 10-hour mentor coach requirement for ACC. Filter by PCC or MCC coaches in DC, education sector, or executive focus. Mentor coaching is not optional — start looking in Phase 1.
Positive Intelligence (PQ / Shirzad Chamine)
positiveintelligence.com
 Framework / Program Growing quickly in executive coaching. The "Saboteur" model resonates with leaders who already know their behaviors but cannot change them. The PQ coaching program is ICF-approved for hours. Emerging alternative to ITC for persistent leadership pattern work. Worth researching in Phase 3.
Fierce Conversations (Susan Scott)
Book and certification
 Framework / Book Pairs naturally with Radical Candor and Five Dysfunctions. Scott's model for interrogating reality and confronting avoidance in leadership conversations. Widely cited by education and nonprofit sector coaches. The certification ($800–$1,200) is worth researching for Phase 3.
National Equity Project — Liberatory Design
nationalequityproject.org
 Framework / Website Equity Lab-adjacent resources with freely available tools for designing equitable systems and practices. The Mindsets for Equity framework is applicable in coaching leaders who need to examine their own beliefs before they can lead systemic change.
Center for Creative Leadership (CCL) Open Programs
ccl.org
 Research / Programs CCL is one of the most cited sources in executive coaching research. Their free white papers and leadership reports are worth subscribing to — they give you the research language that corporate buyers respond to. Their 360 instruments are also widely used in organizational contexts.
The Coaching Psychologist (Journal)
bps.org.uk → TCP
 Journal Peer-reviewed coaching research. For coaches doing organizational work, fluency in what the research says matters to institutional buyers. Worth a quarterly scan starting in Phase 2.
