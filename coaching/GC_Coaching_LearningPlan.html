What You Can Learn From Tai
Leadership Circle Profile certification — it is the psychometric C-suite buyers specifically request, and Tai uses it as her primary tool. Immunity to Change facilitator training (Minds at Work) — one of the most rigorous frameworks for persistent leadership barriers. Tai holds both. You should too by Q2 2027.
Where You Are Ahead
Organizational scale at VP level with P&L ownership. Active federal client work (Deloitte/USPS). Facilitation credential path (CPF) — Tai does not hold an IAF credential. Policy and public sector depth (Harvard MPP / UChicago). Broader sector reach: education + government + nonprofit + private.
What Top C-Suite Coaches Have in Common
The coaches who command premium rates and federal or corporate contracts consistently hold multiple credentials (ICF PCC or MCC plus at least one psychometric certification), deploy named frameworks rather than just good questions, maintain clear niche positioning, and have documented organizational outcomes — not just client testimonials. The goal is not to replicate anyone else's path. It is to build a credential and framework stack that is both authentic to your experience and legible to institutional buyers.

05 · Reading List
Core Library for Coaching Practice
Read in order of the phases above. The right book at the right moment in your practice lands differently than reading ahead of experience. Nine books across one year is one every six weeks — that is the right pace.
Co-Active Coaching
Kimsey-House, Kimsey-House, Sandahl & Whitworth
Phase 1 · July 2026
The foundational text behind the Co-Active model. Read it in Phase 1 — before you accumulate habits from other frameworks. One of the most cited books in ICF-aligned training programs.
The Coaching Habit
Michael Bungay Stanier
Phase 1 · August 2026
Seven core coaching questions. Simple, practical, frequently referenced. Read it fast — one sitting. Know the vocabulary before your clients bring it up.
Strengths-Based Leadership
Tom Rath & Barry Conchie
Phase 1 · September 2026
Companion to CliftonStrengths — links strengths to follower needs and leadership outcomes. Essential for making your StrengthsFinder debriefs land with senior leaders, not just as personal discovery.
What Got You Here Won't Get You There
Marshall Goldsmith
Phase 2 · October 2026
Core reading for stakeholder-centered coaching. Understand the behavioral change model before you study the full Goldsmith methodology. This is the book your C-suite clients will have already read.
Immunity to Change
Robert Kegan & Lisa Laskow Lahey
Phase 2 · November 2026
The most rigorous framework for persistent leadership barriers. Read it before you take the facilitator training. Understanding the theory first makes the training land at a different level.
Dare to Lead
Brené Brown
Phase 3 · January 2027
Widely referenced by executive clients. Know the vocabulary — shame resilience, rumbling with vulnerability, armored vs. daring leadership. You will hear it in sessions whether you assigned it or not.
Multipliers
Liz Wiseman
Phase 3 · February 2027
Widely used framework in education and nonprofit leadership coaching. Know it cold — Multiplier vs. Diminisher is a lens many of your clients will find immediately useful and self-implicating.
Leadership and Self-Deception
The Arbinger Institute
Phase 3 · March 2027
Powerful for clients stuck in self-justifying patterns. Written as a narrative, not a textbook — accessible and frequently cited. The "in the box / out of the box" framing is immediately applicable in coaching sessions.
The Work of Leaders
Grenny, Patterson et al.
Phase 4 · May 2027
Companion to Everything DiSC Work of Leaders — relevant when you eventually add DISC to your toolkit. Also valuable standalone as a framework for vision, alignment, and execution coaching conversations.
