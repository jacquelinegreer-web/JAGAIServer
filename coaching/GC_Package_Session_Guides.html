Phase-by-Phase Guide
01
Opening + Grounding (0–10 min)
What to Do
Welcome them warmly. Note that you are glad they are here and that 90 minutes is enough time for something real to happen.
Brief them on the session structure — what to expect, what is confidential, that there is no right answer.
Ask a grounding check-in before moving to the presenting topic.
Opening Script
"Before we get into what brought you here — I want to ask: how are you arriving today? Not necessarily in relation to the topic — just, what is true for you right now?"
Listen to the answer fully. Do not rush past it. What they say here tells you a lot about their state and their style.
02
Their World (10–30 min)
What to Do
Ask what brought them to coaching today — the specific situation, question, or moment that made them book this session.
Listen without interrupting. Take sparse notes. Let them exhaust the surface-level telling first.
Reflect back what you hear, using their exact words where possible.
Identify the thread underneath: what is really going on here?
Key Questions
"What brought you here today — the specific thing that made you think this was the time?"
"I heard you say [phrase]. Tell me more about that."
"What would you most want to be different about this situation?"
03
Deepening (30–50 min)
What to Do
Move beneath the presenting topic. This is where GROW or CLEAR takes over as a loose guide.
Ask what they already know but have not said out loud yet.
Notice what they are avoiding. Name it gently if it is safe to do so.
Slow the pace here. Silence is useful. Let the deeper material surface.
Key Questions
"What do you already know about this that you haven't fully let yourself know yet?"
"What is the thing beneath the thing you just described?"
"If the answer were already in you — and I think it is — what would it be?"
04
Core Coaching (50–75 min)
What to Do
This is the heart of the session. The real work lives here. Stay present, stay curious, do not rush to solve.
Challenge with care when you see a pattern, contradiction, or belief that is keeping them stuck.
Offer observations rather than interpretations: "I notice..." not "I think you are..."
Trust that the client will find the clarity if you hold the space well enough.
Key Questions
"I want to reflect something back — and tell me if this lands. I noticed that every time we get close to [X], something shifts. What is true about that?"
"What would you do if you gave yourself permission to do what you actually want to do?"
"What does this situation need from you that only you can give?"
05
Clarity Moment (75–85 min)
What to Do
Ask them to name what has shifted or clarified. Use their words, not yours.
A good clarity statement is specific, true, and often involves something they had not fully said before.
Write it down (in Simply.Coach notes). Reflect it back to them. Let it land.
Ask what question they want to carry with them — one question worth sitting with after the call.
Key Questions
"Looking back at this session — what is the most important thing that happened for you?"
"What is now clear that was not clear when we started?"
"What is a question worth sitting with — one that does not need an answer today?"
06
Close + Action (85–90 min)
What to Do
Ask what one action they will take — not the most ambitious, but the most meaningful.
Confirm their action and the timeline. Keep it specific.
Close warmly. Mention that you will send a follow-up with notes and a check-in.
If appropriate, mention that this work can continue through the Intensive or Deep Dive.
Closing Script
"We have about five minutes. What is the one thing you are taking from today? And what will you do — specifically, by when — because of this conversation?"
After the Call — Within 24 Hours
Send the follow-up email with: the clarity statement they named (in their words), their committed action, the question to carry, and a brief warm close. Offer a 4-day check-in text or email. See Clarity Call Playbook for full templates.

Session-by-Session Guide
S1
Foundation + CliftonStrengths Debrief
Month 1 · Week 1 · 60 min
Purpose
Establish the relationship and the working agreement. Anchor the engagement in the client's strengths. Set 2–3 meaningful goals for 3 months.
Key Questions
"What kind of leader do you want to be known as?"
"Looking at your Top 5 — which theme surprises you most? Which did you already know?"
"When is your leadership at its best? What themes are present in those moments?"
"What would success look like at the end of three months — in your words, not in metrics?"
Frameworks + Close
CliftonStrengths debrief (Top 5 or Full 34) — themes, strengths in context, shadow sides
Set 2–3 coaching goals in Simply.Coach (client's words, not coaching-speak)
Between Sessions
Written reflection: In what situations do your top strengths help? Where do they get in your way? Share 3–4 sentences in email or Voxer before Session 2.
S2
Current Reality
Month 1 · Week 3 · 60 min
Purpose
Get an honest picture of the leadership landscape. Who are the key relationships? What is working and what is not? Identify the one situation most needing attention.
Key Questions
"What is actually happening in your team or organization — not the version you tell your manager, but the honest one?"
"Who in your world are you most uncertain about — and why?"
"If you had to name the one thing getting most in the way of your leadership right now, what would it be?"
Frameworks + Close
Stakeholder mapping (manager level: team + peers + own manager; director/exec: expand the map)
Name the primary coaching challenge: the one situation that represents the larger pattern
Between Sessions
Map your key stakeholders: who is with you, who is neutral, who is uncertain? Note one thing you want to say to each category that you have not said yet.
S3
The Real Work — Core Coaching Challenge
Month 2 · Week 1 · 60 min
Purpose
Go into the primary challenge at depth. This is the heart of the engagement. Do not rush past the discomfort — this is where growth lives.
Key Questions
"What has been sitting with you since our last session?"
"What is the thing about this situation that you haven't fully said out loud to anyone?"
"What would you do if you were not afraid of how it would land?"
"What does staying in the current pattern cost you?"
Frameworks + Close
GROW or CLEAR as the session arc — let the client's energy determine which
Use ITC framing if appropriate: what competing commitment is underneath this?
Commit to one action before Session 4 — specific, uncomfortable, theirs
Between Sessions
Take one action connected to the core challenge. Report back via Voxer or email: what happened? What did you notice?
S4
Skill Building + Framework Session
Month 2 · Week 3 · 60 min
Purpose
Introduce a framework that names what the client is navigating. Give them a model they can carry and use between sessions and after the engagement ends.
Key Questions
"Looking at this framework — where do you most recognize yourself?"
"Which quadrant [or dimension] is your growth edge right now?"
"If you applied this to one real situation before we meet next — what would you do differently?"
Choose Framework by Client Level
Manager: Radical Candor — four quadrants, real conversation role-play
Director: Executive Presence framework + stakeholder influence mapping
Executive: Ripple Effect Mapping — trace the leadership decisions made in the last 90 days
Between Sessions
Apply the framework to one real situation before Session 5. Write 3–5 sentences: what happened, what you noticed, what shifted.
S5
Accountability Review
Month 3 · Week 1 · 60 min
Purpose
Honest review of the whole engagement so far. What has moved? What has not? What does the final stretch need to accomplish?
Key Questions
"On a scale of 1–10, where are you on each of the goals we set in Session 1?"
"What has actually changed about how you lead — not just what you have tried, but what is different?"
"What is the honest answer to why one thing has not moved as much as you hoped?"
"What do you most need from our final session?"
Structure
Pull up the 3 goals from Session 1 in Simply.Coach. Rate progress on each together.
Identify what still needs attention in Session 6. Recalibrate if needed.
Between Sessions
Write a brief self-assessment: What is different about my leadership now vs. when I started? What am I most proud of? What is still unfinished? Share before Session 6.
S6
Integration + What Comes Next
Month 3 · Week 3 · 60 min
Purpose
Full arc review and integration. The client leaves with a named leadership identity, a 30-day plan, and a sense of what they are capable of now that they were not when they started.
Key Questions
"I came as a leader who... I am leaving as a leader who..."
"What is the version of yourself that showed up in this work that you want to sustain?"
"What conditions or practices do you need to keep that growth alive without coaching?"
Structure + Close
Leadership Statement exercise: 2–3 sentences about who they are as a leader, written by them in the session
30-day forward plan: 2–3 specific commitments
Warm close. Offer the pathway to Deep Dive if this work has opened something larger.
Follow-Up (within 48 hours)
Send the final follow-up: their leadership statement, their 30-day plan, and a warm note about the arc of the work. Request a testimonial at Sessions 4–5 — not here.

Month-by-Month Guide
M1
Discovery + CliftonStrengths + 360 Launch
Sessions 1–2
Session 1 Focus
Full CliftonStrengths Full 34 debrief — themes in order, shadow sides, patterns under pressure
Set 2–4 goals for 6 months. More ambitious than the Intensive — include identity goals, not just behavioral ones
Launch the 360: select rater groups, send codes, set 2-week deadline
"What do you want to be true about your leadership at the end of six months that is not fully true right now?"
Session 2 Focus
Deeper CliftonStrengths exploration — how do themes interact? Where do they create internal tension?
Leadership identity mapping: who are you at your best, and what gets in the way?
Confirm 360 responses are coming in; follow up with rater groups
"Tell me about a moment in your leadership when you were most fully yourself. What was present?"
Between Sessions
Written reflection: What patterns show up in your leadership that you are proud of? What patterns show up that you wish did not? This becomes the foundation for the 360 debrief conversation.
M2
360 Debrief + Identity Work
Sessions 3–4
Session 3 — 360 Debrief (Full Session)
Receive the data together. Walk through it methodically: strengths themes first, then development themes
Ask: what surprised you? What confirmed something you already knew? What are you dismissing too quickly?
Note the largest gap between self-rating and rater rating — that gap is usually the most important conversation
"What is the piece of data in here that you do not want to look at? That is where we are going next."
Session 4 — Identity and Brand
From the 360 data: what does your leadership brand say to the people around you? Is it the brand you intend?
Identity question: who are you as a leader, separate from your title, your organization, your results?
Introduce ITC if appropriate: what would have to change for the gap in the 360 to close — and what belief would be threatened by that change?
Between Sessions
Write a draft Leadership Brand Statement: "The leader I am known as is... The leader I want to be known as is..." Keep both honest. Bring it to Session 5.
M3
Strategy + Mid-Point Formal Review
Sessions 5–6
Session 5 — Strategic Leadership
Zoom out: what does the client need to build, change, or stop over the next 90 days at the strategic level?
Stakeholder map update: what has shifted in the key relationships since Month 1?
Leadership strategy: where will they invest their leadership energy for maximum impact in this role?
"If you had to bet on the one thing that would make the most difference in how you lead over the next 90 days — what would it be?"
Session 6 — Mid-Point Review (Structured)
Pull up the original goals. Rate progress 1–10 on each. Write the ratings together.
Client writes a brief self-assessment before this session: what has changed, what has not, what the second half needs
Recalibrate goals if needed. Some original goals may be resolved; new ones may have emerged.
Mid-Point Self-Assessment (Client Pre-Work)
Before Session 6, client writes 1 page: What has shifted in my leadership since Month 1? Where am I still stuck? What do I most need from the second half of this engagement? Share in Simply.Coach.
M4
Influence, Systems, and Equity Leadership
Sessions 7–8
Session 7 — Influence and Political Navigation
Map the political landscape at the organizational level. Who holds informal power? Where does influence actually flow — not as it should, but as it does?
Director/Executive level: the real work is often here — navigating systems and people who are not directly accountable to you
"What conversations are happening about you in rooms you are not in? What do you believe people say about your leadership when you are not there?"
Session 8 — Equity and Organizational Health
Conditions of Belonging as a diagnostic: who has full belonging in the organization this client leads? Who does not?
Ripple Effect Mapping: trace the downstream impact of 2–3 key decisions made in the last 6 months. Who was affected, and how?
The coaching question: "What does your leadership permit — and what does it prohibit, even unintentionally?"
Between Sessions
Equity audit prompt: In the last 30 days, whose voice did you most amplify? Whose did you not hear? What patterns do you notice in who has access to you?
M5
Sustainability + Thought Leadership
Sessions 9–10
Session 9 — Sustainable Leadership Practice
What practices are sustaining this client's growth — and which are not?
Who is in their support architecture (mentor, sponsor, peer coach, therapist)? What is missing?
The executive question: what does rest look like when you are responsible for an organization?
"What would fall apart if you were unavailable for two weeks? What does that tell you about how you have built this?"
Session 10 — Thought Leadership and Voice
What idea, belief, or experience from this engagement is worth sharing beyond this room?
What is the contribution this client's leadership is making to their field, not just their organization?
For executive-level clients: succession thinking. Who is being developed to carry what they have built?
Between Sessions
Write the first draft of one thing you want to put into the world — a LinkedIn post, a talk, a paper, a policy proposal. It does not have to be polished. Bring it to Session 11.
M6
Integration + Transition
Sessions 11–12
Session 11 — Forward Planning
Full arc review. Pull up goals, 360 themes, CliftonStrengths debrief notes, leadership brand statement from Month 2
What is different now vs. Session 1? Be specific.
Build the 12-month forward plan: 3 commitments the client will sustain without coaching
"What do you know about yourself as a leader now that you did not know six months ago? And what are you going to do with that?"
Session 12 — Close and Legacy
The final session. Make it feel like one.
Client reads their leadership brand statement from Month 2 — and writes a revised version based on who they are now
Name what remains. Not every thread gets tied. Some of the best work opens questions that do not resolve.
Offer ongoing engagement options: quarterly check-ins, second engagement, organizational retainer
Follow-Up (within 48 hours)
Send the full arc summary: original goals + progress rating + leadership brand (before/after) + 12-month forward plan + a personal note about the work. This document lives in Simply.Coach and can be shared as a case study (with permission).

Session-by-Session Guide
S1
Community Agreements + CliftonStrengths Group Debrief
Month 1 · 90 min
Structure (90 min)
0–20 min: Introductions + Community Agreements — each person names what they need to be honest in this space
20–50 min: CliftonStrengths group debrief — each person shares their Top 3 and the one theme that most shows up in their leadership
50–70 min: Full group discussion: where do we see overlap? Where do we see tension? What does this cohort collectively bring?
70–85 min: Paired reflection — one person shares what is hard about being a leader at this level; partner listens without fixing
85–90 min: Close — each person names one word that describes what they want from this cohort
Facilitation Notes
Community Agreements are not rules — they are commitments. Ask: "What do you need from this group to show up honestly?" Build the list together.
The Gallup group debrief should be about patterns in context, not definitions. "Tell us about a time this theme showed up in your leadership" is more useful than "here's what this theme means."
Opening question: "What is hard about being a leader at this level that you rarely say out loud — and that you are hoping this group might help you carry?"
Between Sessions
Read each other's top 5 themes (share the list with the group). Note where you see someone else's theme in their leadership before Session 2.
S2
Leadership Identity
Month 2 · 90 min
Structure (90 min)
0–15 min: Check-in — what has shown up in your leadership since last month?
15–40 min: Content input: introduce the Multipliers framework (Multiplier vs. Diminisher). Ask: which lane are you in right now — and when?
40–65 min: Peer coaching triads (rotate every 8 min) — one person shares a leadership identity challenge; two coach using the framework
65–80 min: Full group debrief — what came up across the triads?
80–90 min: Each person names one thing they are going to do differently based on this session
Facilitation Notes
Peer coaching triads are the engine of cohort sessions. Set them up clearly: the "client" shares a real situation in 2 minutes; the "coaches" ask only questions (no advice) for 6 minutes; then switch.
Executive Presence lens for director/executive cohorts: "What does your leadership signal to someone who does not have the context you have?"
For manager cohorts: "What is expected of you as a manager that you were not expecting when you were promoted?"
For executive cohorts: "Who are you as a leader when the title or the organization is removed?"
S3
Difficult Conversations + Candor
Month 3 · 90 min
Structure (90 min)
0–15 min: Check-in + what is one conversation you have been putting off?
15–40 min: Radical Candor four quadrants — present the model, ask where they most recognize themselves and their team
40–65 min: Role-play in pairs — one person plays themselves, one plays the team member in the delayed conversation. Then debrief in the full group.
65–80 min: For director/executive cohorts: Five Dysfunctions — which level is your team stuck at? Peer coaching on one real situation.
80–90 min: Commitments — each person names one conversation they will have before next month's session
Facilitation Notes
The role-play is the most powerful part. Keep it real — use an actual situation, not a hypothetical. The discomfort is the point.
For manager cohorts: focus on peer-to-peer and manager-to-direct-report conversations
For director/executive cohorts: extend to board conversations, peer executive dynamics, and conversations with people who have more formal power
Debrief question: "What did you notice about where you went in Ruinous Empathy — and what would it have taken to stay in Radical Candor?"
S4
Equity + Authority
Month 4 · 90 min
Structure (90 min)
0–15 min: Check-in — what is one thing you have noticed about who has voice and power in your organization?
15–40 min: Conditions of Belonging diagnostic — walk through the three conditions (Valued, Connected, Contributing) as a group. Each person rates their team.
40–65 min: Small groups: who in your organization is not fully in? What would it take to change that?
65–80 min: Full group: what does your leadership signal about who belongs here?
80–90 min: One commitment each — something they will do differently based on this session
Facilitation Notes
This session surfaces real discomfort. Hold it. Do not rush to solutions or reassurance.
The goal is not to make everyone comfortable with equity — it is to make the question of equity a leadership question, not a values question
Use Ripple Effect Mapping with executive cohorts: trace a recent decision and map who it affected, and how
Opening reframe: "This session is not about whether you are a good person. It is about what your leadership makes possible — and what it does not — for people who are not like you."
S5
Influence + Building Your Leadership Profile
Month 5 · 90 min
Structure (90 min)
0–15 min: Check-in — what influence move did you make this month, and how did it land?
15–40 min: For manager cohorts: Multipliers — from solving to developing. What do you need to let go of to grow?
For director/executive cohorts: Thought leadership — what is one idea from your experience that the field needs to hear?
40–65 min: Peer feedback on thought leadership draft — each person shares 60 seconds on one idea; group asks one deepening question
65–90 min: Sustainability and burnout — what is the honest conversation about rest and longevity at this level?
Facilitation Notes
By Month 5, the group has enough trust to go to harder places. Use it.
The thought leadership conversation is not about producing a LinkedIn post — it is about naming what they know that others do not yet say out loud
"What is the thing you believe about leadership at this level that you have not yet said publicly — because it is uncomfortable, or too honest, or because you were not sure it was yours to say?"
S6
Integration + Community Close
Month 6 · 90 min
Structure (90 min)
0–20 min: Full arc check-in — each person completes: "I came as a leader who... I am leaving as a leader who..."
20–50 min: Peer gifting — each person names one thing they received from someone in this cohort that they could not have gotten from individual coaching
50–70 min: Commitments witnessed — each person names one commitment made in front of the group
70–85 min: Closing circle — one word that names what this cohort gave you
85–90 min: Your close — acknowledge what happened here and what they are taking forward
Facilitation Notes
The peer gifting exercise is one of the most powerful closing structures available. It names what only group work can produce — the insight that comes from being witnessed by people who are navigating the same terrain.
Do not rush the closing circle. Let it land.
Your close: "What happened in this room over six months is not replicable. What you built with each other — the trust, the honesty, the willingness to show up — that is yours. I am grateful to have held this space with you."
Follow-Up (within 48 hours)
Send a personal note to each participant naming something specific you observed in their growth. Send the group a summary: what we named together, what we built, what each person committed to.
